# Chapter 2 — Open Resources

Curated free and openly licensed material for going deeper on software development
processes. Legend: 📘 book/book chapter · 🎓 course/courseware · 📄 paper or standard ·
🎥 video/talk.

## Foundational values

- 📄 **Manifesto for Agile Software Development** — the four values and twelve principles that
  define the agile movement, plus the list of original signatories.
  <https://agilemanifesto.org/> · Free to read; the manifesto text is freely reproducible with
  attribution to its authors.

- 📄 **The Scrum Guide (2020)**, Ken Schwaber and Jeff Sutherland — the definitive, vendor-
  neutral 13-page definition of Scrum's roles, events, and artifacts.
  <https://scrumguides.org/scrum-guide.html> · Released under Creative Commons
  Attribution-ShareAlike (CC BY-SA 4.0).

- 📘 **Extreme Programming**, Portland Pattern Repository (C2 wiki) — the original community
  wiki where XP's practices (TDD, pair programming, simple design, continuous integration) were
  discussed and refined. <http://wiki.c2.com/?ExtremeProgramming> · Free to read.

## Plan-driven and risk-driven models (primary sources)

- 📄 **"Managing the Development of Large Software Systems"**, Winston W. Royce (1970) — the
  paper universally cited as the origin of the "waterfall" diagram. Read it carefully: Royce
  presents the strict sequential model and then argues it is *risky* and must be iterated,
  which is the opposite of how it is usually invoked. Widely available as a free PDF (search the
  title). · Free to read (archival reprint).

- 📄 **"A Spiral Model of Software Development and Enhancement"**, Barry W. Boehm (1988) — the
  original, highly readable presentation of the risk-driven spiral framework.
  <https://www.cs.unc.edu/techreports/86-020.pdf> · Free to read (university technical report).

## Course material

- 📘🎓 **Engineering Software as a Service (ESaaS)**, Armando Fox and David Patterson, UC
  Berkeley — Chapter 7 covers agile, Scrum, and process in depth alongside a running project.
  Companion site with free materials, slides, and MOOC links. <https://www.saasbook.info/> ·
  Free companion materials; MOOCs offered free to audit on edX.

- 🎓 **MIT OpenCourseWare, 6.005 / 6.031 "Software Construction"** — open readings and
  problem sets on test-first development, specifications, and designing for change that
  complement this chapter's engineering practices. <https://ocw.mit.edu/> · Creative Commons
  Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike.

- 🎥 **"Agile Product Ownership in a Nutshell"**, Henrik Kniberg — a widely used 15-minute
  whiteboard talk explaining backlogs, prioritization, and the Product Owner role.
  Searchable on YouTube. · Free to view.

- 📘 **"Shape Up," Ryan Singer (Basecamp)** — the free online book behind §2.8: shaping,
  six‑week cycles plus a two‑week cool‑down, betting instead of backlogs, the circuit
  breaker, and hill charts. <https://basecamp.com/shapeup> · Free to read online.
