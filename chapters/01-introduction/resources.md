# Chapter 1 — Open Resources

Free, open‑licensed, or freely accessible materials that reinforce this chapter. Types: 📘 open
text · 🎓 course · 📄 primary source · 🎥 video. Licenses vary and are noted where known.

## What software engineering is

- 🎓 **UC Berkeley — Engineering Software as a Service (ESaaS), ch. 1** —
  [saasbook.info](https://saasbook.info/). Motivates SE and agile from first principles.
- 📘 **MIT 6.031 Software Construction, "Reading 1"** —
  [web.mit.edu/6.031](http://web.mit.edu/6.031/www/sp21/classes/01-static-checking/).
  Frames all of SE around three goals: *safe from bugs, easy to understand, ready for
  change.*
- 📄 **SWEBOK Guide v4 (IEEE Computer Society)** —
  [computer.org body of knowledge](https://www.computer.org/education/bodies-of-knowledge/software-engineering).
  The discipline's knowledge areas at a glance.
- 📘 D. Farley, *Modern Software Engineering* (Addison-Wesley, 2022) —
  [informit.com](https://www.informit.com/store/modern-software-engineering-doing-what-works-to-build-9780137314911).
  The engineer-as-scientist framing cited in §1.1.3. Commercial book (not open access).

## Origins of the discipline (used in §1.1.3)

- 📄 **NASA — Margaret Hamilton** biography —
  [science.nasa.gov](https://science.nasa.gov/people/margaret-hamilton/). Her leadership of
  the MIT Instrumentation Laboratory's Apollo flight-software team. Free to read.
- 📄 **IEEE Computer Society — "Margaret Hamilton: First Software Engineer"** (2018) —
  [computer.org](https://www.computer.org/publications/tech-news/events/what-to-know-about-the-scientist-who-invented-the-term-software-engineering).
  Hamilton, in her own words, on why she pushed the term "software engineering". Free to read.
- 📄 D. Eyles, **"Tales from the Lunar Module Guidance Computer"** (AAS 04-064, 2004) —
  [doneyles.com](https://www.doneyles.com/LM/Tales.html). First-hand account of the Apollo 11
  1201/1202 alarms and the Executive's priority scheduling, by one of the programmers. Free to read.
- 📄 P. Naur & B. Randell (eds.), **NATO Software Engineering Conference report, Garmisch
  1968** (1969) — [cs.ncl.ac.uk PDF](http://homepages.cs.ncl.ac.uk/brian.randell/NATO/nato1968.PDF).
  The conference that named the "software crisis". Free to read (hosted by Brian Randell).
- 📄 G. Moore, **"Cramming More Components onto Integrated Circuits"** (*Electronics*, 1965) —
  [doi.org](https://doi.org/10.1109/N-SSC.2006.4785860). The original Moore's Law paper
  (official IEEE reprint catalog page; PDF may require access).

## Complexity and defects

- 📄 F. Brooks, *No Silver Bullet* (essence vs. accident), *IEEE Computer* 20(4), 1987 —
  [doi.org](https://doi.org/10.1109/MC.1987.1663532) (official abstract page); also widely
  available as a PDF via library and university course pages.
- 📄 E. W. Dijkstra, **"Notes on Structured Programming"** (EWD249, 1969) —
  [cs.utexas.edu EWD archive](https://www.cs.utexas.edu/users/EWD/ewd02xx/EWD249.PDF).
  Source of "testing shows the presence of bugs, never their absence" (p. 7). Free to read.
- 📘 **MIT 6.031** readings on *static checking* and *testing* develop the
  fault‑vs‑failure and black‑box‑vs‑white‑box distinctions used here.
