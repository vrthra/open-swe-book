> **How to use this template.** Copy this file into your team's repository (for example,
> `docs/pitches/pitch-court-scheduler.md`), delete these instruction blockquotes, and
> replace every _italic prompt_ with your own content. **One page maximum.** A pitch is
> not a proposal: it is a problem that is *rough-solved and bounded*
> ([§2.8](../chapters/02-software-development-processes/#28-shape-up-fixed-time-variable-scope)) —
> solved enough to show a feasible path, bounded enough to say what it will *not* do.
> Each teammate pitches at least one idea.
>
> **You are writing for another team.** Under the dual-hat model
> ([Running the Project on Two-Week Sprints](../chapters/appendix-a-team-project/two-week-sprints.md)),
> a *different* team builds this pitch — and your team becomes its **customer**. Write so
> that strangers can build from it, and only pitch something you are willing to answer
> questions about all semester. The developer team completes the full
> [project proposal](project-proposal.md) with you after the swap.

## 1. Problem

_Tell one concrete story of the problem happening to a specific person, and state the
baseline — how they cope today. If you cannot name a real person or office who feels this
pain, pitch something else._

## 2. Appetite

_Why is this worth roughly a semester of two-week sprints — no more, no less? Time is the
fixed box; scope flexes to fit it
([§4.2.4](../chapters/04-requirements-analysis/#424-appetite-fixed-time-variable-scope)).
Say what a "small but real" version looks like._

## 3. Rough solution

_Sketch the core idea at fat-marker level
([§6.1.2](../chapters/06-design-and-architecture/#612-design-includes-architecture)):
elements, connections, and the main user flow — strokes too thick for detail on purpose.
A photo of a whiteboard sketch is ideal._
